# X Threads - Anthropic Frontier Model Shutdown

## Thread 1 - The Three-Day Timeline

1. Anthropic launched Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 on June 9.
2. Fable was broadly available with safeguards. Mythos offered fewer cyber restrictions to vetted defenders.
3. Anthropic described them as its most capable models, with exceptional software and cybersecurity abilities.
4. On June 12 at 5:21 p.m. ET, Anthropic says a U.S. government directive arrived.
5. The reported order covered access by every foreign national, even foreign-national Anthropic employees inside the U.S.
6. Anthropic disabled both models for every customer.
7. The government has not publicly released the directive.
8. Anthropic says the apparent concern involved a narrow jailbreak and minor, previously known vulnerabilities.
9. That is the company's account. The public cannot compare it with the government's complete evidence.
10. Three days after launch, the models were gone. The risk standard remains hidden.

## Thread 2 - What Is Verified

1. What do we actually know about the Fable 5 shutdown?
2. Verified: Anthropic publicly announced the U.S. directive on June 12.
3. Verified: Anthropic said it covered all foreign nationals, wherever located.
4. Verified: Anthropic globally disabled Fable 5 and Mythos 5.
5. Verified: both models launched three days earlier.
6. Verified: Fable and Mythos used the same underlying model with different safeguards.
7. Verified: Mythos access was limited to vetted Project Glasswing partners.
8. Not verified: the directive's complete text and specific legal citation.
9. Not verified: the complete jailbreak evidence or comparative risk.
10. Not verified: claims that political retaliation drove the decision.
11. A missing public explanation is not proof that no classified evidence exists.
12. It is proof that public accountability remains incomplete.

## Thread 3 - The Policy Contradiction

1. On June 2, the White House ordered greater federal access to frontier AI for cyber defense.
2. The order called for classified benchmarks, pre-release collaboration, and a clearinghouse for AI-discovered vulnerabilities.
3. On June 5, another memorandum ordered faster AI adoption across intelligence and warfighting.
4. It emphasized frontier-model access, diverse suppliers, reliability, control, and availability.
5. On June 12, a government directive led Anthropic to remove its most capable models globally.
6. These policies are not automatically contradictory.
7. A government can promote safe models and restrict one it believes is exceptionally dangerous.
8. But that requires a credible standard applied consistently across providers.
9. The public has not been shown that standard in this case.
10. Frontier AI cannot be governed permanently by private letters and competing corporate statements.
