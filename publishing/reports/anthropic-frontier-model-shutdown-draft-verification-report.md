# Draft Verification Report - Anthropic Frontier Model Shutdown

Reviewed: 2026-06-22

## Executive Review

The 3,626-word draft is a sourced public-record investigation, not original reporting. It accurately centers the strongest confirmed event: Anthropic said a June 12 U.S. export-control directive covered all foreign nationals and led the company to disable Fable 5 and Mythos 5 globally.

The draft consistently attributes claims that originate with Anthropic, distinguishes legal inference from documentary proof, and expressly states that the directive and government evidence are not public. It gives a substantive version of the government's strongest possible national-security rationale and does not present political retaliation, illegality, or technical disproportionality as established fact.

## Editorial Risks

- **Circular sourcing:** Much incident reporting originates with Anthropic.
- **Company self-interest:** Anthropic has financial and legal reasons to minimize the significance of the government's evidence.
- **Classified-evidence asymmetry:** The government may possess material facts unavailable to the public.
- **Technical sensitivity:** Exploit details must remain excluded.
- **Motive attribution:** Retaliation and political punishment must remain framed as unproven possibilities.
- **Terminology:** Avoid calling the action a universal government "ban"; the verified description is a foreign-national access directive that Anthropic implemented through global suspension.
