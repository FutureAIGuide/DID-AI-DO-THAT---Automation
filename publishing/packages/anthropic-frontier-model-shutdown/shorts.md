# YouTube Shorts - Anthropic Frontier Model Shutdown

## Short 1 - The Three-Day AI

**Hook:** One of the world's most capable AI models survived three days.

**Problem:** Anthropic launched Fable 5 on June 9. On June 12, the company says the U.S. government ordered it to block every foreign national from using Fable 5 and Mythos 5.

**Evidence:** Anthropic says the directive arrived at exactly 5:21 p.m. Eastern.

**Twist:** The restriction targeted foreign nationals, but Anthropic disabled the models for everyone.

**CTA:** The government has not released the directive. Read the full evidence breakdown.

## Short 2 - Fable Versus Mythos

**Hook:** These two AI models were actually the same model.

**Problem:** Fable 5 was broadly available with automated safety controls. Mythos 5 removed some cyber restrictions for vetted defenders.

**Evidence:** Anthropic said Mythos-class systems could find and exploit serious software vulnerabilities.

**Twist:** The government directive reportedly covered both versions, despite their different safeguards and access rules.

**CTA:** So what technical standard made both too dangerous? That remains publicly unanswered.

## Short 3 - The Nationality Problem

**Hook:** How does a cloud service know someone's nationality?

**Problem:** The reported U.S. directive covered every foreign national, including people working inside America.

**Evidence:** Location can be estimated through networks. Nationality requires identity and legal-status verification.

**Twist:** Anthropic's immediate answer was not selective blocking. It shut the models down globally.

**CTA:** This may become a precedent for controlling access to frontier AI.

## Short 4 - Government Versus Government Policy

**Hook:** The government demanded more frontier AI, then triggered a frontier-model shutdown.

**Problem:** June White House orders called for faster AI adoption in cybersecurity, intelligence, and warfighting.

**Evidence:** They also called for classified benchmarks and a vulnerability-clearinghouse.

**Twist:** Days later, a private directive led Anthropic to remove its strongest cyber models.

**CTA:** Those policies need one public, consistently applied risk standard.
